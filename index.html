CS 184 Final Project Proposal

Rigid Body Simulation

Summary

We will attempt to implement a 3D, real time rigid body simulation, contingent on our ability to implement a simpler 2D simulation. If we find that it is not feasible to build the full 3D implementation, we will attempt to expand our 2D system to incorporate deformable objects and/or kinetic energy tracking.

Team Members

Jeffrey Shi (jeffster888 at berkeley dot edu); Siyao Ma (claire.ma at berkeley dot edu)

Problem Description

We will be trying to solve the problem of simulating realistic rigid body physics. Primarily, this will involve simulating interactions between fairly complex rigid body objects. We will attempt to implement, in two dimensions, an algorithm which is generalizable to three dimensions-- to simulate geometry, we may attempt to use the particle/voxel-based representation described in the Nvidia resource linked below.

Goals and Deliverables

Planned Deliverables

Implementation of a 2D, real time rigid body simulation, with objects of varying geometry (i.e. more complex than convex polygons) colliding on screen. The simulation should be interactive, and allow for control over gravity. Torque should be accurately simulated as well.  We will provide a video of the demonstration.

Stretch Deliverables

Implementation of a 3D, real time rigid body simulation, as described in the paper linked below. Other avenues of exploration include adding deformable objects and tracking the transferral of kinetic energy between colliding objects.

Metrics

We hope to have frames per second displayed in the user interface.

Analysis

In our analysis, we intend to address the complexities of simulating rigid body physics and incorporating adjustable outside forces, such as gravity. Time permitting, we may also use our simulation to illustrate how kinetic energy is dispersed before and after collisions between objects.
